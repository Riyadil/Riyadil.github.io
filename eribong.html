In K-pop culture, a light stick is so much more than a glow stick for concerts. While it is a practical item to have in dark concert venues, light sticks act more like a symbol of fans’ unity and strength. It enables artists to look out into a crowd and see a sea of their trademark color being emitted from light sticks they have created. Although they typically have the same purpose, artists put in the effort to add a special twist to their light sticks, whether it be using a specific color, type of aesthetic, or certain shape.

ERIBONG

Eribong came from the word Bong – meaning light, and ERI, the nickname for EXO-L's. Echoing the love of EXO-L's version 1, 2 and 3 Eribongs proved to bring home unique charms for each of its owners creating a lasting memory every time the power button is turned on.

What makes EXO’s lightstick special is not only the silver color – a specific color of EXO-L, but it can also glow up to 12 different colors. Also because of this special ability, every time fans are forced to scan tickets or lightsticks to enter a concert, the system will automatically identify your location in the crowd and glow in the right colors in order to create the most colorful blocks in tune to create a beautiful symbol of the SM’s artists.


ERIBONG : VERSION 1

The first version of the official EXO light stick was announced on October 7, 2015 through the official page of EXO. It was exclusively prepared for the upcoming first concert of the group titled Love Concert In Dome. The concert was held on October 10, 2015 at the Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul. Only the people who bought the ticket and attended the event could acquire the light stick - it was not available for pre-order nor purchase elsewhere. The fans could get three other collectibles at the concert including a set of 18 postcards, a set of 9 photo cards and a luminous button of a diameter of 50 mm. The Version 1 of the EXO light stick was manufactured in China and is an official merchandise from SM Entertainment.

The item has a black head in a hexagonal shape featuring the logo of the group and a black handle with a button showcasing the band's name. The version 1 of the EXO light stick can glow in white color and can operate three modes including On, Blink and Flicker. It is powered by three AAA alkaline batteries similarly to other light sticks. Its expected maximum working time is 5 hours. The product comes with a black warranty card with a hologram logo of EXO, a hand strap and quick start guide. You will get all the contents inside a very elegant box which opens on one side. It's equipped with a magnetic clasp. The dimensions of the product are 102 x 38.9 x 272 mm.


ERIBONG : VERSION 2

Version 2.0 seems to be of much better quality than the original. The coolest new feature of Version 2.0, though, is how it synchronizes to the stage lighting at concerts. To do this, you just sync up the lightstick to your concert ticket using your smart phone.

The new version definitely seems to have been an improvement over version 1.0. Although they are the same size, the new one feels more solid and heavier and has a much better mechanism for screwing in the base. By base, I mean the part that you take off to put the batteries in.For version 1.0, it just twists about half a turn into place. But, that makes it easy for it to fall out by accident. Version 2.0, by contrast, actually twists around several times so that it gets screwed into place firmly and stays there. Version 2.0 also comes with a silver colored base that you can switch out if you want some variety or something different from the white. The light in the head of the light stick of version 2.0 is also brighter and whiter than its predecessor. They have the same functions as far as blinking. The both blink fast and slow as well as just stay on without blinking.


ERIBONG : VERSION 3

The recent Asian EXO Planet 5 brought the same rave, enthusiasm and excitement to all EXO-L’s even after realising that the group is performing with less than 3 members; Lay, DO and Xiumin. SM Entertainment made sure that the fans were fuelled with equally exciting merchandise for the much awaited affair. Crowning the spotlight is the version 3 of the Eribong, the official lightstick of EXO’ls.

In general, the version 3 lightstick version is now the new favourite having the most beautiful light modes among the 3 versions plus the bluetooth feature which allows the automated control during concerts without patching to any applications. Battery feature is also technically advance with longer battery life and it comes with a battery pack where the owners can change batteries any time saving on charging time specially when the action is most needed.
